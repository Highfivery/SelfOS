Privacy, by architecture

Your inner life shouldn't live on someone else's server.

SelfOS has no backend and no account. Everything you write stays in encrypted files in a folder you control. The only thing that ever leaves your device is the specific message you send to Claude when you choose to use an AI feature.

Encrypted at rest

Private content is encrypted with a master key only your devices hold — protected by a recovery phrase and an owner PIN.

Plain files you own

No proprietary database. Your data is portable, inspectable, and yours — back it up or move it like any other folder.

Sharing means "informs their AI"

In a household, shared context can help a partner's coach — but it's never shown to them, and you scope it by relationship type.

Good questions

The things people ask first.

Is SelfOS therapy?
No. SelfOS is a wellness and self-help tool — it is not a medical device, not therapy in the clinical sense, and not a substitute for professional care. It draws on therapeutic frameworks to help you reflect, but it doesn't diagnose or treat. If you're in crisis, contact local emergency services or a crisis line.
Is my data really private?
Yes. There's no backend and no account. Your content lives in encrypted files in a folder you choose, on your own machine. The only outbound network calls are the messages you send to the Claude API (with your own key) and a lightweight version check to GitHub — which sends no personal data and can be turned off.
Do I need a Claude API key? What does it cost?
SelfOS itself is free and open. The AI features use your own Anthropic (Claude) API key, billed directly to your Anthropic account — there's no subscription to us. SelfOS includes per-person and household budgets, prompt caching, and a usage view to keep costs visible and in check.
Which platforms are supported?
macOS today (the build is currently unsigned, so first launch needs a one-time Gatekeeper bypass — see the README). An iPhone companion that shares the same vault over iCloud Drive is in the works. Windows and Linux are later phases.
Can my partner or household use it too?
Yes. SelfOS supports multiple people and the relationships between them. Each person gets their own coaching, and you choose exactly what context is shared — scoped by relationship type. "Shared" means it can inform the other person's AI, but is never shown to them directly.
Is it open source?
The source is public on GitHub, built spec-first and slice by slice. You can read every line, build it yourself, and see exactly how your data is handled.
